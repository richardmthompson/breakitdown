# After: Using `/breakitdown`

## User Request
"Help me analyze my day structure and optimize for productivity"

User invokes: `/breakitdown`

## AI Response (With `/breakitdown`)

### Step 1: Initial Context
I'm going to analyze your day structure and help optimize it. To do this effectively, let me break this into sequential checkpoints so we can tackle one thing at a time.

[TodoWrite created with 5 checkpoints]

### Checkpoint 1: Understanding Current Blockers
**First, let me clarify one critical question:**

Is the context engineering framework currently blocking the VoxPlan release?

---

**User:** "Yes, it is."

---

### Checkpoint 2: Job Search Activities
**Got it. Second question:**

What's the 0.5hr job search block in your schedule - is this CV updates or active applications?

---

**User:** "CV updates and LinkedIn outreach."

---

### Checkpoint 3: Time Breakdown Analysis
**Perfect, that helps. Now let me share the time analysis:**

Here's your current breakdown:
- Deep work: 4 hours (25%)
- Meetings: 3 hours (19%)
- Email/Slack: 2 hours (13%)
- Context switching: 1.5 hours (9%)

**Key finding:** Your deep work blocks are fragmented (three 80-min sessions instead of focused 2-hour blocks). This costs you ~45 minutes daily in ramp-up time.

**Question:** Should I show you the meeting distribution analysis next, or would you like to discuss fixing the deep work fragmentation first?

---

**User:** "Let's fix the deep work first."

---

### Checkpoint 4: Deep Work Optimization
**Smart choice. Here's the recommendation:**

**Current:** Three 80-minute sessions at 2 PM, 4 PM, 6 PM (suboptimal cognitive hours)

**Proposed:** Two 2-hour blocks at 6-8 AM and 9-11 AM (peak cognitive performance)

**Why this works:**
- Morning = highest cortisol (alertness)
- Eliminates 45 min/day of ramp-up waste
- Protects your best cognitive hours

**Trade-off:** Requires shifting your morning meetings.

**Does this structure work for you?**

---

**User:** "Yes, but how do I handle the morning meetings?"

---

### Checkpoint 5: Meeting Restructuring
**Great question. Here's the meeting strategy:**

**Current problem:** Meetings cluster 9-12, creating "swiss cheese" schedule

**Solution options:**
1. **Batch to afternoons** (1-4 PM Tuesday/Thursday)
2. **Convert to async** where possible (status updates, quick questions)
3. **Implement "No-meeting Wednesday"** for protected deep work

**Which approach fits your team culture best?**

---
